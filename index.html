Overview

The Cubic Postcode system divides space into a 21,000,000 × 21,000,000 × 21,000,000 grid of 1m³ cubes centered at Earth's center of mass using ECEF coordinates. Each location gets a unique, unambiguous 22-digit code.

Total cubes: ~9.261 × 10²¹. Supports prefixes with dash for autocomplete.

Reasoning & Fool-Proof Design

  • Layering along X-axis: Alternating positive/negative for contiguous numbering.
  • YZ Square Spiral: Projection using max(|y|,|z|)+1 with careful corner and side calculations.
  • Reversibility: Forward and inverse functions must match within 0.5m.
  • Fixed bugs from original Python/JS: corner offsets, rounding, ECEF formulas, big integer handling.
